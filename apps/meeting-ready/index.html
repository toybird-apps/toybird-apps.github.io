macOS meeting-readiness utility

Meeting Ready

Avoid last-minute surprises before an occasional Zoom Workplace call.
Check update guidance, devices, and the settings you may need before the meeting starts.

Support Privacy Policy

Check Zoom Workplace update guidance

Meeting Ready compares the installed Zoom Workplace version with public guidance published by Zoom, including minimum and prompted versions.

Test meeting devices before the call

Preview a camera, check microphone input, and play a short speaker test sound only when you choose to run a diagnostic.

Get ready before a scheduled Zoom call

Optional calendar integration checks the next 14 days locally and can remind you before detected Zoom meetings when attention may be needed.

Useful when Zoom is not your everyday meeting app

Occasional external meetings

If your daily work happens in Google Meet or Microsoft Teams, a rarely used Zoom Workplace install may be out of date when an external meeting arrives.

A small menu-bar companion

Meeting Ready can stay available in the menu bar and run lightweight checks without recording or storing meeting content.

Clear settings guidance

macOS manages camera, microphone, screen-recording, and accessibility permissions per app. Meeting Ready opens the relevant settings so you can visually confirm Zoom Workplace.

Public version information

The app checks public Zoom HTTPS pages for published version guidance. It does not use private Zoom APIs or attempt to reproduce every organization-specific update rule.

Local calendar processing

If you enable calendar integration, Meeting Ready searches for Zoom links locally on your Mac. Calendar titles, notes, attendees, URLs, meeting IDs, and passcodes are not sent to the developer.

Independent app

Meeting Ready is an independent app and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or provided by Zoom Communications, Inc.

Private by design

Meeting Ready does not require an account, include advertising, use analytics SDKs, or track users.

Camera video, microphone audio, screen contents, and meeting contents are not saved or transmitted. The app uses HTTPS only to retrieve public Zoom version guidance.
